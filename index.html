Matilda Richardsson

About

Hi! My name is Matilda and I'm an experienced animation programmer at Avalanche studios. I have a Degree of MSc in Interactive Media Technology with focus on Computer Graphics from KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Stockholm, Sweden. My main interests in animation programming started when I went on an exchange semester at KAIST in South Korea. At KAIST I took a course in Motion Graphics, which opened up my eyes to animation programming and was the reason I chose to do my master thesis within the field and also later to work with it.


When I'm not working you will most likely find me skiing, hanging out with friends or sitting at home watching tv-series or reading. I also enjoy painting, singing, playing guitar or going on long walks and hiking. I overall just love the nature and animals :D

Released work

Emerald Coast is a DLC for The Hunter:Call of The Wild. For this DLC I worked on the kangaroo as well as working on improving the swim logic I added in Missippi Acres Reserve.
New England Mountains is a DLC for The Hunter:Call of The Wild. I mostly worked on a complete remaster of the moose in the game. Additionally I worked on improving all animals navigation logic to make them more stable and better at following the AI.
Revontuli Coast is a DLC for The Hunter:Call of The Wild. For this DLC I worked on updating and refactoring the waterfowl birds as well as working on the aggressive states on the capercaillie bird.
Mississippi Acres is a DLC for The Hunter:Call of The Wild. I worked on adding logic to the game, allowing animals to swim at different speeds in different depths which was added to the alligators in the game. I also worked on the first animal to interact with objects in the world where we added so that raccoons can enter and eat from trash bins.

Other projects

My master thesis for Most efficient Inverse Kinematics algorithm for Quadruped models: Comparing FABRIK to CCD that was done at KTH Royal Institute of Technology.
TravellAR was developed in a group project, and is a co-op augmented reality experience that is best described as a puzzle exploration game.
Viscous Biscuit is a puzzle platformer game developed as a group project in the course Computer Game Design.
Pokemochi is a multiplayer mobile game developed as a group project in a course at KTH.
Embodied assistant is a research project at KTH, where an audio assistant was implemented into a furhat.
